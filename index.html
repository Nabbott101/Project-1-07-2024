The name Tennessee comes from Tanasi, a Cherokee town in the eastern part of the state that existed before European settlement. The American government called the land the Territory South of the River Ohio before it became a state. Tennessee was initially part of North Carolina, and later the Southwest Territory, before its admission to the Union as the 16th state on June 1, 1796. It earned the nickname "The Volunteer State" early in its history due to a strong tradition of military service.
